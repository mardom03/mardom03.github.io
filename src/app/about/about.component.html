About Me

I am a software engineer and technology consultant focused on opportunities in New York City, with a background in computer science, mathematics, and large-scale platform development. I recently graduated from the University of Wisconsin-Madison with a B.S. in Computer Science & Mathematics, where I combined a strong technical foundation with leadership roles across campus organizations.

Alongside my academic work, I spent several years in production engineering roles spanning platform engineering and web application development, with exposure to security validation in production environments. I built and maintained .NET-based backend systems, automated CI/CD pipelines, developed internal tooling supporting hundreds of developers, and supported web application vulnerability remediation efforts. I am particularly interested in work at the intersection of engineering execution, risk management, and business outcomes.

In addition to my technical work, I have led large, cross-functional teams in non-technical environments. As a director of Humorology, UW-Madison's annual Greek philanthropic theater production, I coordinated over 100 students and helped raise more than $400,000 for Wisconsin charities. I have also worked in public-sector environments as a Legislative Intern at the Wisconsin State Capitol, supporting policy research and constituent services across a range of complex issues.

I am drawn to roles that require structured problem-solving, clear communication, and ownership from concept through delivery. Professionally, I am focused on opportunities in technology consulting and software engineering, with a long-term interest in technical leadership and management. Outside of work, I enjoy weightlifting, golf, and reading.
